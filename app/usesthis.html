The Setup

This is what I use to get stuff done

Hardware

  • Ikea Markus
    High-end desk chair from Ikea.

  • MacBook Pro
    (Retina, 13-inch, Mid 2014), work computer. This is where most of the software development gets done. I usually have it attached to an external Dell display at the CartoDB office.

  • MacBook Air
    (11-inch, Late 2010), home computer. I bought it with my first payroll. Best computer I ever had.

  • Dell S2309W
    When it comes to displays I always trust Dell. This was the lower-end screen I could afford at the time and has accompanied me since then.

  • Logitech Anywhere MX
    I'm used to touchpad gestures, but I easily jump to the mouse for some tasks. This is the only wireless mouse I've come to like, after a couple of years. I just can't Magic Mouse. — Amazon

  • Audio-Technica ATH-M50
    Red goes faster. After some research these were some of the best over-ear headphones, and they are worth every dollar. — Amazon

  • Samsung Galaxy S4
    I've learnt to know I don't really need the latest iPhone.

  • Garmin Fenix
    Classic ABC, plus GPS. One day I'll wear a Speedmaster Professional Moonwatch. — Amazon

  • TomTom Multi-Sport
    I crashed my Garmin Fenix, so meanwhile I have it fixed I bought the cheapest watch for multisports training. I miss advanced interval training setup, but it's good value for money. — Amazon

  • Kindle Paperwhite
    I bought it right after using one during my last holiday trip. — Amazon

Software

Beware, entering the Nerd zone.

  • Sketch
    Haven't paid for a personal license yet. I'm still broken-hearted after supporting version 2, but we fully use it at CartoDB for design.

  • Sublime Text
    Coming from the now Open Source software TextMate, Sublime Text is the fastest and simplest code editor I've used. From time to time I give Atom a try but I still don't feel comfortable. I use it with Spacegray theme, and Source Code Pro font. The only vim command I know is :wq.

  • iTerm 2
    Along with ohmyzsh. Chruby for Ruby (and Rubygems) version management. I use Grunt and Bower for my Front-end tooling workflow, and Jekyll for building static sites (like this). Virtual Machines live in vagrant.

  • Google Chrome
    The usual stuff here, I spend half of the day in DevTools. I try to keep it low on extensions: Blipshot for screenshots (Awesome screenshot was better but I don't like some of their practices), Earth View for new tabs (my favourite extension ever), Google Cast as a companion for the Android Chromecast app, JSONView (there are savages out there who read plain JSON responses), LastPass for password management, and uBlock to prevent tracking. I use bookmarklets in the bookmarks bar to send webpages to Pinboard and Instapaper.

  • Other software and services
    There's a bunch of free and subscription-based software I use on a daily basis: ImageOptim, Spectacle, GitHub, Gmail, Slack, Wunderlist, Simplenote, Pinboard, Digg Reader, Instapaper, Strava, Spotify, Netflix, Plex, et cetera.

Gear

As I started training for triathlon in late 2015, I thought it could be interesting to add a section with the gear that I use.

Swimming

  • Arena swimsuit, goggles, and swim cap
    Basic equipment for swimming pool. I'm not doing any openwater yet, so no wetsuit.

Bike

  • Scott Sportster X40
    Urban bike to ride in the city, and the occasional trail in Casa de Campo. I want to buy a road bike for the next season and I'm already considering a Scott Speedster 20 or a Canyon Endurace AL 6.0.

  • Gore Bike Wear Element Bib Tights
    Very confortable and warm on cooler days. I have some other spare tights but you can tell the difference in terms of the quality of the fabric. — Amazon

  • Gore Bike Wear Element Windstopper Soft Shell Jacket
    I love this soft shell jacket. It fits perfectly and protects from wind, and light rain. — Amazon

  • Endura Xtract long sleeve
    I usually wear this under the jacket in cold weather, or when temperatures are low in the morning. — Chain Reaction Cycles

  • Cold weather gloves
    5€ pair of gloves from Decathlon.

Running

  • Running t-shirt
    Both long and short sleeve t-shirts from races, depending on the temperature, and time of the year.

  • Kalenji running tights
    I just have one pair but they wash and dry very quickly. I have had them for years.

  • Kalenji trail running shoes
    My first running shoes, since I started running continuously. I think I will replace them soon.

I've bought the previous products online from Amazon, Wiggle, Chain Reaction Cycles, and Decathlon, and I'm very happy so far with the products and the shipping.

Colophon
